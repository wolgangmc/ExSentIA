2.1.1   Choosing the right level of data isolation

    You can choose to isolate resources in your deployment at the domain level or at the tenant level. If you isolate at the domain level, then all domains in the tenant can share resources and all use the same transaction counting. If you isolate at the tenant level, then resources are not shared between the domains in Tenant A and the domains in Tenant B. Instead, you must transport the resources between the DAS database in Tenant A and the DAS database in Tenant B. With tenant-level isolation, transactions are counted separately for each tenant.

    At a high level, if your business requires strict data isolation between business groups—such as would be necessary in the healthcare or financial services industries—then you should use separate tenant environments for each business group to keep sensitive data completely separate. If, however, different groups in your OpenText Communications environment need to collaborate closely as they work—such as with separate groups who are developing and testing the same applications—then you might use multiple domains within a single tenant to separate those groups. Whatever the scenario, OpenText Communications provides the tools necessary to customize your deployment to meet your specific business needs.
