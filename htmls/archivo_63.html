2.3.2.7   Linking domains to auto-associate resources

    When you configure a domain in a tenant, you can link other domains to it if they are in the same tenant. Linking the other domains allows all approved resources in the configured domain to be automatically associated with the other linked domains.

    If a domain always shares all of its approved resources with other domains, then linking domains to it can be more efficient than associating individual resources to that domain.

    Example 2.17: Linked domains in a hierarchy

    Suppose that an OpenText Communications deployment has the following domain hierarchy and domain links:

    • Corporate domain

      • Division A domain: linked to Corporate domain

        • Business Unit A1 domain: linked to Division A and Corporate domains

        • Business Unit A2 domain: linked to Division A and Corporate domains

      • Division B domain: linked to Corporate domain

        • Business Unit B1 domain: linked to Division B and Corporate domains

        • Business Unit B2 domain: linked to Division B and Corporate domains

    In this case, approved content in the corporate domain is auto-associated with the division domains and each division’s business unit domains. The auto-associated corporate content can be used in communications in all division and business unit domains, but it cannot be changed.

    Likewise, if content is approved in a division domain, then it auto-associates only with its business unit domains. The business unit domains can use the content from their division domain, but they cannot update it or share it with business units in other division domains.

    In addition, automatic and manual resource association can work together. For example, a business unit in Division A can share some of its resources with all the domains in Division B if you manually associate those resources with Division B. When the resources are approved, Division B auto-associates them to all of its business units.


    When you configure domain links in Tenant management view, a domain association map shows the domain that you are linking to as the “current domain.” The map also shows domains that are “associated to” and “associated from” the current domain.

    The domain association map defines domains that are associated to and from the current domain as follows:

    • Domains that are associated to the current domain automatically receive updates to their resource associations when content is approved in the current domain.

    • Domains that are associated from the current domain automatically send resource association updates to the current domain when content is approved in those domains.

    Note

    OpenText Communications prevents the configuration of circular domain link chains, for example, Domain A ⇒ Domain B ⇒ Domain C ⇒ Domain A.

    In order for a domain to auto-associate its resources to another domain, the other domain must have the permissions required for the resource types that are being associated. For more information about the permissions required to access resources, see Section 2.3.1 “Configuring roles and permissions”.

    To apply auto-association links to a domain:

    1. In the OpenText Communications web client, sign into the following URL as a tenant administrator user:

      https://<host>:<optionalPort>/design?tenant=<tenantName>

    2. To open Tenant management view, click the Tenant management button .

    3. In Tenant management view, click Domains.

    4. In the Domains panel, click the domain that you want to auto-associate to other domains.

    5. In the properties panel for the domain, on the General tab, verify that the Production check box is cleared.

    6. In the properties panel for the domain, select the Associations tab.

    7. Beside the Domain association map, click the Edit automatic domain associations button .

    8. In the Automatic domain associations dialog box, in the Available domains list, click the add button beside each domain that you want to move to the Applied domains list and link to the current domain.

    9. Click Apply.

    10. Optional In the Domain association map, click a domain name button in the Associated to column or the Associated from column and then do the following:

      1. Repeat step 7 through step 9 to link available domains to the selected domain (which now appears in the Current domain column).

      2. Optionally, repeat step 10 to select another domain and then link available domains to it.

    11. Click Save.

    To remove auto-association links from a domain:

    1. In the OpenText Communications web client, sign into the following URL as a tenant administrator user:

      https://<host>:<optionalPort>/design?tenant=<tenantName>

    2. To open Tenant management view, click the Tenant management button .

    3. In Tenant management view, click Domains.

    4. In the Domains panel, click the domain that you want to remove auto-association links from.

    5. In the properties panel for the domain, on the General tab, verify that the Production check box is cleared.

    6. In the properties panel for the domain, select the Associations tab.

    7. Next to Domain association map, click the Edit automatic domain associations button .

    8. In the Automatic domain associations dialog box, in the Applied domains list, click the remove button beside each domain that you want to move to the Available domains list and unlink from the current domain.

    9. Click Apply.

    10. Optional In the Domain association map, click a domain name button in the Associated to column or the Associated from column and then do the following:

      1. Repeat step 7 through step 9 to remove applied domain links from the selected domain (which now appears in the Current domain column).

      2. Optionally, repeat step 10 to select another domain and then remove applied domain links from that domain.

    11. Click Save.
