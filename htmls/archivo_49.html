2.2.6   Deleting tenants

    You use System management view to remove a tenant from your OpenText Communications deployment. If your deployment uses automatic schema management, then you can fully delete a tenant—that is, deleting the tenant not only removes the association between your deployment and the tenant schemas in your database, but it also deletes the tenant schemas from your database. If your deployment does not use automatic schema management, however, deleting a tenant in System management view simply removes the association between your deployment and the tenant schemas in your database. After you remove the association, a database administrator must then manually remove the tenant schemas from the database. Additionally, if you are using automatic schema management in your deployment, and you delete the sample tenant, it will reappear after you restart the Design pod.

    Keep in mind that you can delete a tenant for an individual service, while leaving the corresponding tenants in place for the other services in your OpenText Communications deployment. For example, you might want to delete a tenant if something goes wrong with the tenant schema in your OpenText Communications database. Or you might even make tenant deletion a regular part of your development process if you use particular tenants for testing during each release cycle. After a release cycle ends, you can delete and recreate a tenant for each service in your deployment so that you start with a completely clean tenant at the beginning of the next release cycle.

    For more information about working with Interactive tenants, including how to use the tenant management API calls, see OpenText Communications - Interactive Services and API Online Help (CCEE250100-H-AGD)OpenText Communications - Interactive Services and API Online Help (CCEE250100-H-AGD).

    Deleting tenants in with automatically managed schemas

    If your deployment does not use automatic schema management, then you should use the procedure for deleting tenants in manually managed databases.

    To delete tenants in System management view:

    1. Sign in to the OpenText Communications web client as an OTDS administrator user at the following URL:

      https://<host>:<optionalPort>/design?tenant=system

    2. On the Tenants and services panel, click the indicator for the tenant that you want to delete.

    3. Click Delete, and then click Delete again to confirm deletion.

    4. Click Next or Previous and repeat step 3 to delete the tenant for a different service, or click Cancel to close the Tenant information dialog box.

    Keep the following things in mind when you delete a tenant from your OpenText Communications deployment:

    • If you delete a tenant for a specific OpenText Communications service, but you do not delete the tenant in the other services, the indicator for the deleted tenant in the Tenants list turns gray.

    • If you delete all of the OpenText Communications services but do not delete the service in OTDS, then the tenant no longer appears in the Tenants list, even though it still exists in OTDS.

    Deleting tenants with manually managed schemas

    If you are using automatic schema management for your OpenText Communications databases, then you can use System management view to delete tenants from your deployment. However, if you manually manage your database schemas, then you will need to use System management view to remove the association between the tenant schemas and your OpenText Communications deployment, and then a database administrator will need to manually delete the tenant schemas from your OpenText Communications databases.

    To delete tenants in manually managed databases:

    1. Sign in to the OpenText Communications web client as an OTDS administrator user at the following URL:

      https://<host>:<optionalPort>/design?tenant=system

    2. On the Tenants and services panel, click the indicator for the tenant that you want to delete.

    3. Click Remove, and then click Remove again to confirm removal of the association between the tenant and your OpenText Communications deployment.

    4. Click Next or Previous and repeat step 3 to remove the tenant for a different service, or click Cancel to close the Tenant information dialog box.

    Important

    The tenant schema still exists in your OpenText Communications database, and a database administrator will need to manually delete it to remove the tenant completely.

    Keep the following things in mind when you remove the association between a tenant and your OpenText Communications deployment:

    • If you remove the association between a tenant and a specific OpenText Communications service, but you do not remove the association in other services, the indicator for the tenant in the Tenants list turns gray for the service from which the association was removed.

    • If you remove the association between the tenant and all of the OpenText Communications services but do not remove the association for OTDS, then the tenant no longer appears in the Tenants list, even though it still exists in OTDS.
