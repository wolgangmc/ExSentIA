3.11   Enabling effectivity for Content Author

    Effectivity lets business users in Content Author send personalized information to customers based on date ranges. Effective dates in Content Author specify a date range during which a version of a message, clause, or section is eligible to be included in customer output. For example, your organization might send a new version of a particular communication out to customers each month. For a communication that would be sent to customers in March, then, business users can specify that the in that communication are eligible to be included in customer output only from March 1 until March 31. That way, content intended for March will not accidentally be included in the customer communication for other months.

    Additionally, this functionality helps your organization manage content that is subject to governmental regulation by ensuring that expired or ineligible content does not get included in customer output.

    You can set effective and expiration dates on messages, clauses, and sections in Design asset library view. Business users will be able to set or edit effectivity dates in Content Author.

    Some important considerations when using effective dates with content

    • You can set effective and expiration dates on a piece of content, but you do not need to set both. For example, you might specify an effective date for a piece of content but not an expiration date. In that case, the content would become eligible for inclusion in customer output on the effective date, but the content will not expire.

    • If the effective dates on sections and the clauses that they contain do not match, then whole sections can be inadvertently excluded from customer output. For example, if you set an expiration date on a section, but a clause within the section has an earlier expiration date, then the whole section will be excluded from customer output starting on the expiration date of the clause.

    • When there are multiple versions of a piece of content, the latest approved version with qualifying effective dates will be used in customer output. If there is not a qualifying version of a piece of content, then customer output cannot be created.

    • If you are creating Content Author communications from templates that were created in Designer and you want to use the Designer effective dates in the Content Author communications, then you must enable effectivity for Designer. If you do not, then Content Author will ignore any effectivity settings in content that comes from Designer.

    Enabling effectivity for Content Author

    Note that effectivity is enabled by default for Content Author. If you want to turn off this functionality, you can use these instructions to clear the Use effectivity check box in step 4.

    To enable effectivity for Content Author:

    1. In the OpenText Communications web client, sign into the following URL as a tenant administrator user:

      https://<host>:<optionalPort>/design?tenant=<tenantName>

    2. To open Tenant management view, click the Tenant management button .

    3. In the Configurations panel, click Content Author.

    4. On the General tab of the Properties panel, select the Use effectivity check box.

    5. Click Save.
