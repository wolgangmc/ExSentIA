3.10   Enabling effectivity for Designer

  • Orchestration asset library view
  • Monitor view
  • Rationalization asset library view
  • Dashboards view

    Effectivity lets designers in Designer send personalized information to customers based on date ranges. Effective dates in Designer specify a date range during which a version of a document, design, or component is eligible to be included in customer output. For example, your organization might send a new version of a particular communication out to customers each month. For a communication that would be sent to customers in March, then, business users can specify that the documents and designs in that communication are eligible to be included in customer output only from March 1 until March 31. That way, content intended for March will not accidentally be included in the customer communication for other months.

    Additionally, this functionality helps your organization manage content that is subject to governmental regulation by ensuring that expired or ineligible content does not get included in customer output.

    You can set effective and expiration dates on documents, designs, and components in Design asset library view. Designers will be able to set or edit effectivity dates in Designer.

    Some important considerations when using effective dates with content

    • You can set effective and expiration dates on a piece of content, but you do not need to set both. For example, you might specify an effective date for a piece of content but not an expiration date. In that case, the content would become eligible for inclusion in customer output on the effective date, but the content will not expire.

    • If you are creating Content Author communications from templates that were created in Designer and you want to use the Designer effective dates in the Content Author communications, then you must enable effectivity for Designer. If you do not, then Content Author will ignore any effectivity settings in content that comes from Designer.

    • When there are multiple versions of a document, design, or component, the latest approved version with qualifying effective and expiration dates will be used to create customer output. If there is no qualifying version of the document, design, or component, then the customer output cannot be created. You should remove any expired content from a communication if you plan to send the communication to your customers after the expiration date of that content.

    Enabling effectivity for Designer

    Note that effectivity is disabled by default for Designer.

    To enable effectivity for Designer:

    1. In the OpenText Communications web client, sign into the following URL as a tenant administrator user:

      https://<host>:<optionalPort>/design?tenant=<tenantName>

    2. To open Tenant management view, click the Tenant management button .

    3. In the Configurations panel, click Designer.

    4. On the General tab of the Properties panel, select the Use effectivity check box.

    5. Click Save.
